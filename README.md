Executive Summary
This document presents a comprehensive proposal for the design, development, and deployment of a professional public-facing website for The Cornerstone Foundation. The proposed solution encompasses a modern, professionally designed online presence integrated with a content management system, enabling the Foundation's team to independently manage and update site content without technical expertise.

The Cornerstone Foundation's mission — as established in its Declaration of Trust — spans educational scholarships and bursaries at all levels, construction and maintenance of clinics and medical facilities, relief support for the destitute, aged, infirm, widows and orphans, clean water supply and sanitation infrastructure, and assistance for the most vulnerable members of society. The website must reflect this vision with clarity, credibility, and warmth, serving as the Foundation's primary touchpoint for partners, stakeholders, and beneficiaries.

The total investment of Kenya Shillings Sixty Thousand (KES 60,000) covers complete design and development, domain configuration, content management setup, staff training, and thirty days of post-launch technical support. The project will be delivered over a four-week period, targeting completion before the Foundation's planned April launch event. Donation and payment integration will be scoped as a subsequent phase following launch.

Project Objectives
The primary objective of this engagement is to establish a fully functional, credible online presence that enables The Cornerstone Foundation to raise awareness of its work and attract support and funding through digital channels. The platform will serve as the Foundation's primary public communications tool, providing partners and stakeholders with a clear understanding of the Foundation's mission, programs, and impact.

Upon successful completion, the Foundation will possess:

A professional website that clearly articulates the Foundation's vision, mission, and program priorities.
A regularly updated blog and portfolio section for sharing field visits, photos, and activity reports to sustain stakeholder engagement.
A content management system accessible to non-technical trustees and administrators for ongoing updates.
A credible, searchable online identity ahead of the April launch and planned bursary activities.

Phase One: Frontend Implementation — Weeks 1–2
Payment Milestone: 40% — KES 24,000

The developer collects all branding assets, photos, domain details, and written content from the client. The client reviews the live frontend build at the end of Week 2 before backend work begins.

Deliverables: Functional Next.js frontend for all pages, client review and sign-off.

Phase Two: Backend, CMS & Integration — Week 3
Payment Milestone: 30% — KES 18,000

The backend is built and connected to the frontend. This includes the CMS setup and content modelling, PostgreSQL database configuration, API development, and frontend–backend integration. Domain and hosting are configured and internal QA testing is completed across devices and browsers before the staging site is handed to the client for final review.

Deliverables: Fully integrated staging site, configured CMS and backend, QA test report.

Phase Three: Review, Launch & Handover — Week 4
Payment Milestone: 30% — KES 18,000

The client reviews the staging site and provides final feedback. Revisions are incorporated, the site is deployed live on the Foundation's domain, and a CMS training session is conducted for the Foundation's team. All credentials, documentation, and administrative access are transferred.

Deliverables: Live production website, trained team, complete documentation, credentials handover, 30 days post-launch support.
